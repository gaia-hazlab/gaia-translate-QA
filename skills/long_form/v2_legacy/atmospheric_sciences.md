# Atmospheric sciences — Gaia translator skill file

Companion discipline file for the Gaia translator agent. Mirrors the structure of `hydrology.md`, `seismology.md`, `geomorphology.md`. Citation policy: every author-year shortcode in this file is matched by a full canonical citation in the **Foundational references** section at the bottom (with DOI when available). The LLM is instructed to use those citation strings verbatim in generated QAs.

## Core variables and equations

- Primitive equations: hydrostatic Navier-Stokes for a rotating compressible fluid, continuity, thermodynamic energy, water-substance conservation. State variables (u, v, w, T, p, q).
- Potential temperature θ = T (p₀/p)^(R/cₚ); equivalent potential temperature θₑ (moist-static energy proxy).
- Geostrophic balance −fk × u = −(1/ρ)∇p. Rossby number Ro = U/(fL).
- Hydrostatic balance ∂p/∂z = −ρg. Buoyancy frequency N² = (g/θ)(∂θ/∂z).
- Clausius–Clapeyron: dp_sat/dT ≈ Lᵥ p_sat / (R_v T²) → saturation vapor pressure rises ~7%/K. The thermodynamic anchor of intensifying precipitation extremes [Held & Soden 2006; Trenberth 2011].
- Radiative transfer: τ optical depth, longwave/shortwave fluxes, two-stream approximations.
- Reanalysis "truth" variables: 2 m T, 10 m wind, total column water vapor (TCWV / IWV), precipitation rate, surface pressure.

## Methods

- In-situ: radiosondes (NWS network, 2×daily), surface ASOS/AWS, eddy-covariance flux towers (AmeriFlux/FLUXNET) — same instruments hydrology relies on for ET and ecology for NEE.
- Remote: GOES-R series geostationary imagers, polar orbiters (Suomi NPP, JPSS), MODIS/VIIRS, GPM/IMERG precipitation, AMSR-2, NEXRAD Doppler radar (WSR-88D), GNSS-derived precipitable water (Bevis et al. 1992).
- Reanalysis: ERA5 (Hersbach et al. 2020), MERRA-2 (Gelaro et al. 2017), CFSR.
- Models: GFS, ECMWF IFS, WRF (Skamarock et al. 2008), MPAS, CESM/CAM, CMIP6 multi-model ensembles.
- Data assimilation: 3D/4D-Var, EnKF (Evensen 1994), particle filters — directly shared with hydrology and paleo-reconstruction work. Greg Hakim's group at UW is canonical here.

## Phenomena studied

- Atmospheric rivers (Pacific NW: ~30–50% of annual precipitation, vast majority of major flood events) [Ralph et al. 2018, 2019].
- Extratropical cyclones and convective systems; tropical cyclones; mesoscale convective systems.
- Heatwaves, droughts, flash droughts; the 2021 PNW heat dome.
- Monsoons (NAM, ISM).
- Convective storms, lightning, hail, tornadoes.
- Boundary layer dynamics; orographic precipitation enhancement (key for Cascades and Mt. Rainier hydrology).
- Long-term climate change signals: warming, intensifying hydrologic cycle, shifting storm tracks.

## Translation hooks (where atmospheric science meets the Gaia disciplines)

- **→ Hydrology**: precipitation is the hydrologic forcing. ARs drive flood + landslide hazards. Reanalysis gives spatially distributed forcing where gauges are sparse. Penman-Monteith ET (the agricultural skill) needs atmospheric drivers.
- **→ Seismology**: atmospheric pressure / wind loading is a known seismic noise source (Wang et al. 2017 Reviews of Geophysics on environmental seismology). Microseisms (5–20 s) are generated by ocean swell driven by storms (Longuet-Higgins 1950).
- **→ Geomorphology**: storm-triggered landslides, post-fire debris flows, AR-driven sediment pulses to rivers.
- **→ Geotechnical engineering**: extreme precipitation events drive embankment failures, retaining-wall pore pressure rises, slope movement triggering.
- **→ Ecology**: GPP and phenology are driven by temperature, VPD, precipitation timing.
- **→ Agricultural sciences**: forecast-driven irrigation scheduling, drought monitoring, climate-change projections for crop suitability.
- **Methodological unification**: data assimilation. The atmospheric community matured 4D-Var/EnKF; hydrology, seismology (waveform tomography), and digital-twin frameworks all import this machinery.
